First Mile Advantage of Frameworks and Languages

New frameworks and languages come out every other day. They come out promising the world, increased productivity, increased programmer happiness, less bugs and a clear cut business advantage in speed. They gather steam, the hype cycle starts, tutorials surface, conferences get set up, twitter starts raving and the framework seems to take over the world, only to later slide into obscurity after a while.

First Mile Advantage

Most frameworks advertise and concentrate on their first mile advantage. How quickly you can start writing a greenfield product out the door. This alone is responsible for the hype cycle. Simple things that used to take three days just gets done in 3 seconds. Wiff some generators here and there and boom you have a working app that you can showcase to your boss and get promoted fast.

This is called the First Mile advantage. How quickly your framework or language can cover the first part of the product development. This might involve setting up, and seeing the first point on the screen.

Your Project is a Marathon

Your project is not getting through the first mile. At the first mile your project is indistinguishable from the other projects. It's the last mile that counts and creates the difference both in your project and your business. When you optimize for the first mile, you start to give up flexibility down the road, slowing you down. You have to consider your project as a marathon.
